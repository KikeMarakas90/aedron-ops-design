# Design Patterns

## Mechanisms for Maintaining Coherence

These patterns are not generic best practices.

They are structural mechanisms designed to prevent the breakdown of decision systems under complexity.

All patterns in this section are derived from:

→ [Organizational Coherence](/aedron-ops-design/conceptual-frameworks/organizational-coherence.html)

---

Design patterns in this lab focus on how systems remain:

- stable under volatility  
- aligned across domains  
- governed at scale  
- executable in real-world conditions  

They operate at the level of **implementation logic**, translating architectural intent into operational behavior.

---

## Role of Design Patterns

Patterns exist to address specific failure modes of fragmented systems:

- misaligned KPIs across domains  
- disconnected data-to-action flows  
- inconsistent decision criteria  
- breakdowns in execution under pressure  

Each pattern defines a **repeatable mechanism** to restore or preserve coherence.

---

## Patterns in Development

- **Operational Stability Pattern (v1)**  
  Ensuring consistent system behavior under volatility and operational stress.

- **Multi-Domain Interoperability Pattern (v1)**  
  Aligning decision logic across domains and systems.

- **Governed Data Activation Pattern (v1)**
